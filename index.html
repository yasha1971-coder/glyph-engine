GLYPH

Deterministic byte-exact retrieval over static corpora.

No ranking. No embeddings. No interpretation.

GLYPH is an experimental retrieval engine for exact byte matches at scale.

Why it exists

Most retrieval systems trade certainty for flexibility. GLYPH does the opposite: same bytes in, same matches out.

Core guarantees

Byte-exact substring retrieval
Deterministic results
No semantic interpretation
Sentinel-safe FM-index construction

Architecture

raw bytes
  ↓
suffix array
  ↓
BWT
  ↓
FM-index
  ↓
exact retrieval

Benchmark signal

HDFS 1GB, 100 exact blk_* lookups: 

grep repeated scan:        11.516 sec
GLYPH persistent FM:       0.001673 sec total
average:                   0.0167 ms/query

Experimental prototype. Not a grep replacement for one-off scans. Trades RAM and offline indexing for fast repeated exact queries over static corpora.
